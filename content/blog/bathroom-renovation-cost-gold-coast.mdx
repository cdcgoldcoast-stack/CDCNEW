## The Quick Numbers

I get asked about bathroom costs more than anything else, and the honest answer is that no two bathrooms are the same. In over 25 years of renovating bathrooms on the Gold Coast, I've seen jobs range widely depending on what's hiding behind the walls and what the homeowner wants to achieve. The numbers below reflect what my team and I are actually seeing on our projects right now.

A bathroom renovation on the Gold Coast typically costs between **$20,000 and $55,000** depending on size, scope, and materials. A basic ensuite refresh sits at the lower end. A full main bathroom with custom tiling, freestanding bath, and premium fixtures pushes toward the top.

Here's how it breaks down.

## Cost Breakdown by Category

### Waterproofing ($2,500 – $5,000)

This is the most important line item. Poor waterproofing leads to structural damage, mould, and costly repairs down the track. Queensland law requires licensed waterproofing with a minimum 7-year warranty — we provide 10.

Every dollar here is money well spent.

### Tiling ($4,000 – $12,000)

Tiles are the biggest visual element in any bathroom. Cost depends on tile selection, layout complexity, and how much coverage you need.

- **Budget option:** Large-format porcelain in a simple layout
- **Mid-range:** Feature wall with contrasting tiles, herringbone or subway patterns
- **Premium:** Natural stone, full floor-to-ceiling tiling, custom mosaics

> **Worth knowing:** Tile labour costs as much as (or more than) the tiles themselves. Choosing a simpler layout can save thousands without compromising the look.

### Fixtures and Fittings ($3,000 – $10,000)

Tapware, showerheads, toilet, vanity basin — these are the pieces you touch every day.

- **Matte black and brushed nickel** remain the most popular finishes on the Gold Coast
- A wall-hung toilet saves space and makes cleaning easier
- Frameless shower screens cost more but transform the feel of the room

### Vanity and Cabinetry ($2,000 – $8,000)

A floating vanity with stone top is the Gold Coast standard. Custom vanities cost more but maximise storage in awkward spaces.

For smaller bathrooms, a wall-mounted vanity creates visual space and makes the room feel larger than it is.

### Plumbing ($2,500 – $6,000)

If you're keeping the same layout, plumbing costs stay predictable. Moving a toilet, shower, or basin means re-routing pipes — and that's where costs climb.

### Electrical ($1,500 – $3,500)

Heated towel rails, exhaust fans, LED mirrors, and downlights all need proper electrical work. Most Gold Coast bathrooms need an upgraded exhaust fan at minimum — humidity is relentless here.

## Small Bathroom vs. Large Bathroom

## What Drives the Price Up

- **Moving plumbing locations** — keeping the same layout saves $3,000–$5,000
- **Full-height tiling** vs. half-height can double the tiling bill
- **Custom niches and shelving** — functional but add labour time
- **Natural stone** — beautiful, but costs 2-3x more than porcelain
- **Underfloor heating** — a luxury that adds $1,500–$3,000

## What Drives the Price Down

- **Keeping the existing layout** — the single biggest money saver
- **Large-format tiles** — fewer cuts, faster install, lower labour
- **Standard vanity sizes** — off-the-shelf is significantly cheaper than custom
- **Choosing quality mid-range fixtures** — the price jump from mid to premium is steep, and the quality gap is small

## The Costs Nobody Mentions

- **Skip bin hire:** $300 – $600 for demolition waste
- **Temporary bathroom arrangements:** Plan for 2-4 weeks without your main bathroom
- **Contingency (10%):** Old bathrooms hide surprises behind walls — allow for it
- **Building permits:** Required if you're changing the structure

## Gold Coast-Specific Considerations

Living on the Gold Coast means dealing with humidity, salt air, and hard water. This affects material choices:

- **Fixtures:** Choose marine-grade or coastal-rated tapware if you're near the beach
- **Ventilation:** Oversized exhaust fans are essential, not optional
- **Tile grout:** Epoxy grout resists mould far better than cement-based in humid environments
- **Glass:** Coated shower screens repel water marks and stay cleaner
