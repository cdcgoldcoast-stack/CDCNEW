## The State of Gold Coast Renovations in 2026

After 25-plus years renovating homes across the Gold Coast, I've watched costs shift through every cycle -- from the pre-GFC boom through to the post-COVID spike. My team and I have learned that the only way to give homeowners useful numbers is to pull them from real projects we've actually delivered, not industry estimates that don't reflect what's happening on the ground here in South East Queensland.

The Gold Coast renovation market has stabilised compared to the post-COVID surge, but material and labour costs haven't come back down. Trade availability has improved — you're no longer waiting six months for a tiler — but skilled tradespeople still command fair rates, and building materials remain 15-25% higher than 2019 levels.

What does that mean for your budget? It means realistic expectations are more important than ever. The numbers below are based on actual projects we've delivered across the Gold Coast in the past 12 months — not industry averages or wishful thinking.

## Kitchen Renovation Costs

A kitchen is typically the most complex room in your home. Plumbing, electrical, gas, cabinetry, benchtops, splashbacks, appliances — every element adds to the cost.

| Scope | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Cosmetic refresh (new doors, benchtop, splashback) | $15,000 – $30,000 |
| Mid-range full renovation (same layout) | $35,000 – $55,000 |
| High-end full renovation (layout changes) | $55,000 – $80,000 |
| Premium/luxury kitchen | $80,000 – $100,000+ |

A full kitchen renovation includes demolition and waste removal, all cabinetry, engineered or natural stone benchtops, splashback, appliance supply and installation, plumbing and electrical work, painting, and project management.

**What pushes kitchen costs higher:** Moving the sink or cooktop adds $3,000-$8,000 for plumbing and gas relocation. Structural changes like removing a wall for open-plan living require engineering, steel, and council approval. Premium materials — natural stone benchtops, imported tiles, custom timber cabinetry — all carry a significant premium. A butler's pantry addition, hugely popular on the Gold Coast right now, adds $15,000-$30,000 depending on size and fit-out.

> **Worth knowing:** Keeping your existing kitchen layout is the single biggest cost saver. If your current plumbing and electrical positions work, you can get a stunning result for significantly less than a full relocation.

## Bathroom Renovation Costs

Bathrooms are smaller spaces, but they're labour-intensive. Waterproofing, tiling, plumbing, electrical — there's a lot happening in a tight area.

| Scope | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Budget-conscious renovation | $20,000 – $28,000 |
| Mid-range renovation | $28,000 – $40,000 |
| High-end renovation | $40,000 – $55,000 |
| Luxury/spa bathroom | $55,000 – $75,000+ |

**Where the money goes:** Waterproofing runs $2,000-$4,000 (non-negotiable — must comply with AS 3740). Tiling costs $3,000-$8,000 depending on tile size and complexity. Quality tapware sets run $800-$3,000, freestanding baths start around $2,000, custom vanities run $2,500-$6,000 (wall-hung styles are the Gold Coast favourite), and plumbing and electrical work adds $3,000-$6,000 depending on how much needs moving or upgrading.

When we strip a Gold Coast bathroom, we regularly find water damage in the subfloor (especially pre-2000 homes), outdated plumbing that doesn't meet current Australian Standards, asbestos in wall sheeting or vinyl flooring (pre-1990 homes), and insufficient fall to floor waste drains. A 10-15% contingency budget covers these surprises without derailing the project.

## Whole-Home Renovation Costs

Renovating an entire home is where costs vary the most. A cosmetic refresh of a three-bedroom Robina home is a completely different project from a full gut-and-rebuild of a 1970s split-level.

| Scope | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Cosmetic refresh (paint, flooring, fixtures) | $50,000 – $100,000 |
| Mid-range renovation (kitchen, bathrooms, living) | $100,000 – $200,000 |
| Major renovation (structural changes, full refit) | $200,000 – $350,000 |
| Premium whole-home transformation | $350,000 – $500,000+ |

These ranges cover the Gold Coast market for typical three- to four-bedroom homes. Larger homes, heritage considerations, or complex sites (steep blocks, difficult access) push costs higher.

> **Tip:** For whole-home renovations, Concept Design Construct recommends a phased approach if budget is a concern. Tackle the kitchen and main bathroom first, then stage the remaining work. This spreads the cost and lets you live more comfortably during the process.

## Laundry Renovation Costs

Laundries are often overlooked, but a well-designed laundry adds genuine value — especially on the Gold Coast where outdoor living means more towels, more sports gear, and more washing.

| Scope | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Basic refresh (new cabinetry and benchtop) | $8,000 – $12,000 |
| Full renovation | $12,000 – $20,000 |
| Premium laundry with butler's area | $20,000 – $25,000 |

Key cost drivers include custom cabinetry, stone benchtops, tiling, and whether you're relocating plumbing for appliance repositioning. A well-planned laundry renovation is also one of the best value-adds for Gold Coast families — it's a relatively modest investment that makes daily life noticeably better.

## Outdoor and Deck Costs

Gold Coast living is outdoor living. We spend more time outdoors than most Australians, and decks, patios, and outdoor kitchens are consistently some of the highest-return renovations you can do in this market.

| Scope | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Timber deck (25-40m²) | $15,000 – $35,000 |
| Composite deck (25-40m²) | $20,000 – $45,000 |
| Covered patio/alfresco | $30,000 – $60,000 |
| Full outdoor kitchen | $25,000 – $80,000 |

**Gold Coast consideration:** Salt air is brutal on the wrong materials. Treated pine decking might last five years near the coast before it starts to deteriorate. Hardwood or quality composite decking costs more upfront but lasts 15-25 years with minimal maintenance. If you're in Burleigh, Palm Beach, or anywhere east of the highway, invest in coastal-grade materials.

## Apartment Renovation Costs

Renovating a Gold Coast apartment has its own cost profile. Access logistics, body corporate rules, and working within existing structural constraints all factor in.

| Scope | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Apartment kitchen renovation | $35,000 – $65,000 |
| Apartment bathroom renovation | $22,000 – $50,000 |
| Full apartment renovation (2-bed) | $80,000 – $180,000 |

Apartment renovations on the Gold Coast often cost **10-20% more per square metre** than equivalent house renovations. Restricted working hours (typically 7am-5pm weekdays), lift access scheduling, floor protection requirements, waste removal logistics, body corporate approval timelines, and higher insurance requirements ($10 million public liability is standard) all contribute to the premium. Broadbeach and Surfers Paradise apartments have particularly strict body corporate requirements. ## What Drives Renovation Costs Up

Understanding cost drivers helps you make smarter decisions:

- **Structural changes** — removing walls, raising ceilings, or altering rooflines requires engineering, steel, and council approval. Budget $5,000-$20,000 per structural modification.
- **Plumbing relocation** — every metre you move a drain or water line adds cost. Keep wet areas where they are if possible.
- **Premium materials** — the gap between mid-range and premium can be 50-100%. A Caesarstone benchtop might cost $4,000 where natural marble costs $8,000.
- **Council approvals** — City of Gold Coast development applications cost $1,000-$5,000 in fees and take 4-8 weeks.
- **Site conditions** — steep blocks, narrow access, or asbestos presence all add to the bottom line.

## Where to Save Without Compromising Quality

Not every dollar needs to go toward premium finishes. Smart Gold Coast homeowners save money in these areas without sacrificing the overall result:

- **Keep the existing layout** — this is the single biggest money saver in any renovation. If your kitchen plumbing and electrical positions work, don't move them.
- **Choose engineered stone over natural stone** — similar look, lower cost, easier maintenance in our humid climate
- **Mid-range appliances** — a $3,000 oven cooks the same meals as a $6,000 oven for most families. Spend the difference on better cabinetry instead.
- **Standard tile sizes** — a well-chosen 300x600 tile can look just as elegant as large-format for significantly less in installation costs
- **Vinyl plank flooring** — modern luxury vinyl plank is waterproof, durable, and ideal for Gold Coast humidity at a fraction of hardwood cost
- **Reuse what works** — if your existing bathtub, structural framing, or subfloor is in good condition, keep it. Not everything needs replacing.

## Hidden Costs Most People Miss

These are the line items that rarely make it into the Pinterest mood board budget, but they're real costs that catch people off guard:

- **Temporary living arrangements** — budget for takeaway meals and laundromat visits during a whole-home renovation, or factor in short-term rental costs if you move out. This can run $2,000-$5,000 for a kitchen renovation alone.
- **Council and compliance fees** — building certification, development applications, and inspection fees add up quickly. City of Gold Coast fees vary by project scope.
- **Asbestos removal** — licensed removal runs $2,000-$10,000 depending on extent. Extremely common in Gold Coast homes built before 1990 — found in wall sheeting, eaves, vinyl flooring, and even texture coats.
- **Electrical switchboard upgrades** — older homes often need a new switchboard to handle modern load requirements ($2,000-$4,000)
- **Landscaping and driveway repair** — heavy vehicles and skip bins can damage driveways and gardens during construction
- **Window treatments** — new rooms often need new blinds or curtains. A full house of quality blinds runs $3,000-$8,000

## Gold Coast-Specific Cost Factors

The Gold Coast isn't Sydney or Melbourne, and pricing doesn't translate directly from other markets. Factors unique to our region include:

- **Salt air material requirements** — coastal suburbs need corrosion-resistant fixings, marine-grade hardware, and materials that won't deteriorate in salt-laden air. This adds 5-10% to material costs for properties near the beach.
- **Council requirements** — City of Gold Coast development applications and building certifications have their own fee schedules and processing times that affect project costs and timelines.
- **Access and logistics** — hinterland properties on steep blocks, homes with narrow driveways, and apartment buildings all present access challenges that increase delivery and labour costs.
- **Subtropical climate** — waterproofing, ventilation, and moisture management are more critical here than in drier climates, requiring higher specification materials and more thorough installation.
